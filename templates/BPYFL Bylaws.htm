  1. THE LEAGUE

 

(a) The Bergen Passaic Youth Football League (“The League”) shall consist of communities joined together for the purpose of promoting Junior Football.  Only the highest standard of sportsmanship and conduct are expected of players, coaches and others associated with the game.  Deliberate violation of The League rules is indefensible and will break down rather than aid in the building of the character of the players.  All coaches will abide by the letter and the spirit of the BPYFL Coaches Code of Ethics (see Appendix C).

 

(b) The League was registered on May 9, 2011, as “THE BERGEN PASSAIC YOUTH FOOTBALL LEAGUE.”  The Certificate of Formation was filed with the Bergen County Clerk, pursuant to New Jersey Statute Title 15 “An Act to incorporate associations not for pecuniary profit”, and the Certificate of Formation was duly filed in the Bergen County Clerk’s Office on May 9, 2011.

 

          (c) The League shall schedule an eight-game regular season to determine its division champions and eight (8) playoff participants.  Any consolation games are considered to be sanctioned regular season games, and are subject to all rules governing the playing of games as set forth in subsequent sections of these rules.

 

          (d) The League shall be divided into two (2) conferences.  Each member program must enter one (1) “A” team in the Senior division, one (1) “A” team in the Junior division and one (1) “A” team in the Pee-Wee group.  The League President, or a duly appointed representative of the Executive Committee, will award trophies to the Super Bowl winners in each conference.

 

(e) All programs must have a Head of Organization and League Representative appointed by the March meeting.  At all official League functions, such as meetings and weigh-ins, the authorized Head of Organization, League Representative or a Head Coach shall be in attendance. In their absence, a representative appointed by the Head of Organization or League Representative assuring that the representative person is responsible for official league functions, such as voting on League business, certifying the freeze list at weigh-ins or picking up League schedules, shall attend.

2. ADMINISTRATION

 

          (a) The League shall be governed by an Executive Committee consisting of a President, Vice President, Secretary, and Treasurer, to be elected at the January meeting by 2/3 majority vote of The League membership.  The President shall seek nominations for all Executive Board and Competition Committee positions at the December season’s end league meeting.

 

          (b) Excluding the President, the Executive Committee shall have at least one (1) member from each existing League division or conference.  A program may not have more than one (1) representative on the Executive Committee. The President will vote only in the case of a tie.  In any grievance against an Executive Committeeman’s team, he shall forfeit his right to vote to the President.  The Executive Committee will have the power to modify the divisions and/or schedule in the event that a program withdraws from the league.

 

          (c) A five (5) member Competition Committee will be formed for the purpose of assisting the Executive Committee in matters of the administration of The League.  The Competition Committee shall be convened at the behest of the Executive Committee to review and make recommendations in areas of League business, including but not limited to rules interpretations, disputes and disciplinary hearings.

 

          (d) All teams in The League must advise the President in writing of their intention to participate in League play for the following season no later than the February meeting.  Each league program member shall be assessed a six hundred and fifty dollar ($850.00) league fee per year starting April, 2011, payable at the February meeting to cover the cost of conducting league business. Any member town that has not paid in full their league fees (or submitted a voucher as required by their governing organization) by the April meeting shall begin to accrue a $50.00 fine for each month that the fees are not paid.  The Treasurer shall give a Financial Report at each meeting. 

                                                                            

          (e) Each member town shall have one vote in any given voting situation. The official spokesman for each town member shall be the Head of Organization, League Representative or Head Coach in any official discussions.  Any member town that has not paid their league fees (or submitted a voucher as required by their governing organization) by the June meeting will forfeit their voting privileges until such time as their league fees plus penalties are paid in full.

3. NEW MEMBERS

 

          (a) Applications for membership into The League must be submitted in writing to the President.  All membership applications are to be submitted as soon as possible, but no later than the meeting on the third Thursday in April.  All requests for admission into The League must be voted on and approved by a two-thirds (2/3) majority for review and consideration by The League.

 

          (b) The Competition Committees shall examine the applications as to sponsorship, equipment, playing field and sufficient squad strength. The committee shall report at a general meeting their findings.

 

          (c) A two-thirds (2/3) majority vote of the total membership shall be required to admit new members.

 

          (d) All applications for new membership that request a combination of communities shall be considered.

 

      (e) All new members accepted into The League shall be placed on an administrative probation for one (1) year from the date of their acceptance into The League and will not have voting rights for that period.

 

 

 

                                                                                                                  

4. EXPULSION OF MEMBERS

 

          (a) If evidence of a rules violation by any member town is presented to the President, he will call a meeting of the Executive Board and the Competition Committee within seventy-two (72) hours of said notification to rule on the same.

                                                                                                                                                                            

          (b) The Executive Board and Competition Committee will examine the evidence presented and the member town under investigation shall be given full opportunity to present a defense of the allegations.  The Executive Board and Competition Committee shall report all findings to the membership at an open meeting that shall be convened at the earliest possible opportunity.

                                                                                                                  

          (c) A two-thirds (2/3) vote of the total membership, excluding the violating program, will be necessary to expel a member town from The League.

 

          (d) Any town member expelled from The League may re-apply for membership no earlier than one (1) year from the date of their expulsion.  The League reserves the right to deny the re-applicant membership without cause.

5. ELIGIBILITY

 

          (a) Senior Group - a player must not have reached his 14th birthday before January 1st of the playing year.  Players entering the ninth grade (9th) are ineligible.  Birthday can not be prior to 1/1/1999.

 

          (b) Junior Group - a player must not have reached his 12th birthday prior to January 1st of the playing year.  Birthday can not be prior to 1/1/2001.  Players entering the seventh (7th) grade are ineligible

 

          (c) Pee Wee Group - a player must not have reached his 10th birthday by January 1st of the playing year.   Birthday can not be prior to 1/1/2003.  Players entering the fifth (5th) grade are ineligible.

 

 

          (d) Residence - a player must reside or attend school in the community or communities that he/she represents.  If a player lives in a community that is a member of The League, but attends school in another community, that person must play for their resident community program.  If a player attends school in a League member town, but lives in a community which does not have a Junior Football program, that person may play for the town that they attend school in.  All rosters must contain the complete name and address, including town, for each applicant.  The League shall also require a note from the student-athlete’s Principal certifying that the subject student is a registered student in that school, using a copy of the Student Verification Form (see Appendix D-1)                                                 

 

 

          (e) Freeze Lists - Each program will develop their freeze list based on the number of players that they have on that level.  The frozen players will be restricted to the "A1" team for that level and can not play in any games other then "A1" games only.  All other players on that level are free to play in any other games on that level for his respective program.

6. CERTIFICATE OF ELIGIBILITY

 

          (a) On the Saturday following Labor Day, team coaches must weigh each squad member in the presence of the Weigh-In Committee.  A second (make up) weigh-in will be held on the Wednesday following the Saturday after Labor Day.  This weigh-in shall be for those players unable to attend the first weigh-in, or who failed to make the respective line or backfield weights at the first weigh-in.  New players may be added to the roster up to the second weigh-in after Week 3. 

 

(b) A player may be weighed naked or with shorts (if necessary) at the discretion of the player.  A Birth Certificate or Passport only will be presented for each player to the Weigh-In Committee and there shall be no late submissions permitted.  In addition, two (2) identical Photo Cards for each player shall be presented to the Weigh-In Committee with complete typed information including current photos of each player.  Photos used in previous years are not acceptable for re-use.  At the time of weigh-in, each team shall produce two (2) copies of the official League roster form, fully completed and signed by the Head Coach and all Assistant Coaches.  The roster will also be presented to the Weigh-in Chairman at the assigned time of arrival at the Wednesday make-up weigh-in.  In addition, proof of team insurance shall also be presented in accordance with Rule 2 (g).  Each player will be permitted to step on the scale more than once, at the discretion of the Weigh-in Chairman.

Players listed in HOME jersey number order (no duplicate numbers, attempt number assignment by position)

9. PLAYING RULES

 

The official National Football Federation Rule Book shall be the rule guide for all sanctioned League games with the following exceptions, which shall be provided to each official assigned to League games:

 

(a) Free substitutions shall be allowed.

 

(b) Three (3) time outs in each half shall be allotted to each team.

 

(c) The game shall consist of four quarters.  Each quarter will be ten (10) minutes for Senior and Junior games only.  Each quarter in Pee-Wee games are nine (9) minutes.  All National Football Federation rules shall apply as to the running of the clock.  Each offensive team shall have thirty (30) seconds to snap the ball once the official signals the ball ready for play.

 

(d) Time out for any and all injuries shall be charged to the officials.  When time out is called for injury, the player or players involved must retire from the game for at least one (1) play.

         

(e) The 40-yard line shall be midfield. The kicking team shall kickoff from their 30-yard line and the receiving team shall place five men between their own 35 and 40-yard lines.  If a ball is kicked out of bounds, and is untouched by the receiving team, the receiving team shall have the option of, 1) penalizing the kicking team five (5) yards and having them re-kick, or, 2) accepting the ball at the out-of-bounds spot, or, 3) accepting the ball on their own 15-yard line.  For kickoffs that go into the end-zone or through the end-zone, the receiving team will take possession on their own 15 yard line.  There is no re-kick option.

 

          (f) The successful point after a touchdown via a run or a pass shall count as one (1) point from two (2) yards out and two (2) points from five (5) yards out. 

 

** New for 2013 – Senior Level only, teams will be allowed to kick an extra point provided that the field has goal posts.  A successful kick will count as one (1) point only.  All teams still have the option to run or pass for the extra point.  If a team elects to kick an extra point, the ball will be snapped from the 2 yard line.  If the kick is blocked, or the snap is muffed and recovered by the defense, it will be ruled a dead ball.  Fakes are not permitted.

 

          (g) The half time intermission shall be fifteen (15) minutes maximum.

 

(h) All minor penalties shall be assessed at five (5) yards.  All major penalties in the run of play shall be assessed at ten (10) yards.  Penalties assessed against the Head Coach or any other member of the coaching staff or against the team sideline (including spectators) for unsportsmanlike conduct shall be assessed at ten (10) yards.

(2)When the score is tied at the end of the fourth period, the referee will instruct both teams to return to their respective team boxes.

(10) The game shall end after each team has had one offensive opportunity.  If the overtime period has not determined a winner, the game shall be declared a tie, with each team receiving one standings point and the applicable strength of schedule values.

12. DIVISION CHAMPIONS

 

(a) Conference champions shall be determined by the results of the eight (8) game schedule at the conclusion of the regular season.

 

(b) The conference champion shall be recognized as the team that has accumulated the most standings points (see Rule 11 (d)) after completing their eight (8) game regular season schedule.  In the event two teams in a division or conference have equal points at the end of the regular season, the following tie-breaker order would be in effect:

 

1)   Head-to-head

2)   Most wins (1)

3)   Best overall record for all 8 games

4)   Total number of wins by teams defeated in all games

5)   Co-champions with both making playoffs.  A coin flip would determine which team would be assigned the first-place seeding and the home game, with the coin flip loser being considered the highest seed among remaining playoff teams.

                                                         

 

In the event of a tie between two or more teams, a tiebreaker procedure shall be implemented.  Ties shall be broken in the following order:

(1)Head-to-Head results between the tied teams

(a) In the case of three (3) or more teams being tied, head-to-head takes into consideration all games played among the tied teams (ie: in a three-way tie, Team A’s record vs. Team B and C, Team B’s record against Team A and C and Team C’s record against Team A and B).

(2)Most wins

(3)Total number of wins by teams defeated in division

(4)Best overall record for all 8 games

In the instance where three or more teams are involved, once a team is eliminated by criteria, the process begins back at step (1) with the remaining teams until two teams remain.  Once the tiebreaker is down to two teams, the process reverts back to the two-team tiebreaker criteria above.

 

(c) In the event that all tiebreak rules are unable to break the tie, then The League shall recognize co-division champions.

 

(d) The division champion shall receive an automatic berth in the post-season playoffs.

APPENDIX A

 

COACHES CODE OF ETHICS

 

          The function of a coach is to develop his players through athletic competition.  An athletic program should enhance academic achievement and never interfere with opportunities for academic success.  Each player should be treated as though they were the coach’s own, and their welfare should be uppermost at all times.  Accordingly, the Bergen Passaic Youth Football League has adopted the following guidelines for all coaches.

 

 

(1) The coach shall be aware that he has a tremendous influence, for either or all, on the development of his athletes and, thus, shall never place the value of winning above the value of instilling the highest ideals of character.

 

(2) The coach shall uphold the honor and dignity of his position in all personal contacts with players, parents, game and league officials and fellow coaches.  The coach shall strive to set an example of the highest ethical and moral conduct.

 

(3) The coach shall by word and action take a positive role in the prevention of drug, alcohol and tobacco abuse by his players.

 

(4) The coach shall know the rules of the game and shall teach them to his players.  He shall not seek unfair advantage by circumventing the spirit or letter of the rules.

 

(5) The coaches of the competing teams should meet in the spirit of good sportsmanship before and after games to set the correct tone for the event.

 

(6) The coach shall exert his influence to enhance the sportsmanship of his players and the spectators.

 

(7) The coach shall support and respect game officials.  The coach shall not indulge in conduct that would incite players or spectators against game officials or opponents.  Public criticism of game officials or opposing players or coaches is unethical and unsportsmanlike conduct.

 

(8) The coach shall not scout opponents by means other than those sanctioned by the Bergen Passaic Youth Football League.

 

Coaches violating the Coaches Code of Ethics shall be brought under review by the Bergen Passaic Youth Football League’s Executive Committee.  Disciplinary actions may range from suspensions to disbarment from further participation in The League.
